on-chain vs off-chain

Across many NFT communities you can hear conversations about what exactly does it mean when NFTs are referred to as being on-chain or off-chain. The distinction between these two concepts is of primary importance and it is essential to understand.

Many people believe in a misconception that everything that uses the blockchain technology leaves the digital assets impervious to corruption, reliable and permanent. While this is true in terms of the smart contracts after their deployment, there is a potential vulnerability that exists intrinsically in the way that some projects store complex data (like digital artwork, images, videos, music, etc.). In the specific, if the data is stored off-chain, then there is a chance that you could be stuck with a smart contract that points to a broken link or a missing file. To become a more informed collector, it is of uttermost importance to understand what are the on-chain and off-chain components of the NFTs projects. These concepts are useful even to understand the quality of a project.

Many projects among NFT communities are made from two distincted parts: the smart contract and the metadata that actually contains the artwork and additional information. The smart contract lies on a blockchain, and contains a set of instructions and functions to facilitate the transaction and serves as a digital description of the content. The smart contract includes a link that points to the server that stores the digital artwork. On the other hand, the digital artwork may not live on a blockchain and may be stored "off-chain" or simply, not on a blockchain. Off-chain storage includes centralized servers within the company or an InterPlanetary File System (IPFS). The issue with centralized storage is that if the server goes down (or the company dissolves) the image would be lost. IPFS is a more secure method of finding data as it utilizes a distributed, decentralized system. While still off-chain, if a storage location (node) goes down in one spot it may be found in another location. Due to the immutable quality of the smart contracts, rewrites to redirect to the image are not possible should there be a disruption to the off-chain method of storage. In this scenario, a collector would be left with a smart contract that contains a link to unavailable content.
